Srinivasa Ramanujan

-- Man who knew Infinity --

Some highlights from the life of Ramanujan:

  • 1887 : Born : 22 December 1887,Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1892 : Enrolled at the local school.
  • 1897 : Passed his primary examinations, stood first in the district(Age 10).
  • 1898 : Had exhausted the mathematical knowledge of two college students(Age 11).
  • 1901 : Was receiving merit certificates and academic awards(Age 14).
  • 1902 : Went on to find his own method to solve the quartice quations.
  • 1903 : Studied the contents of the book, A Synopsis of Elementary Results , which is generally acknowledged as a key element in awakening the genius of Ramanujan.
  • 1904 : Graduated from Town Higher Secondary School, was awarded the K. Ranganatha Rao prize for mathematics.
  • 1905 : Ran away from home, heading towards Visakhapatnam and stayed in Rajahmundry for about a month.
  • 1906 : Failed his Fellow of Arts exam and again a year later. Without a degree, he left college and continued to pursue independent research in mathematics. At this point in his life, he lived in extreme poverty and was often on the brink of starvation.
  • 1909 : Married to a ten-year-old bride, Srimathi Janaki.
  • 1913 : Wrote to G. H. Hardy. Hardy recognised some of Ramanujan's formulae but others "seemed scarcely possible to believe". Hardy wrote a letter to Ramanujan, expressing his interest for his work. Hardy contacted the Indian Office to plan for Ramanujan's trip to Cambridge. Ramanujan refused to leave his country to "go to a foreign land".
  • 1914 : Apparently, Ramanujan's mother had a vivid dream in which the family Goddess commanded her "to stand no longer between her son and the fulfilment of his life's purpose". Ramanujan then set sail for England.
  • 1916 : Was awarded a Bachelor of Science degree.
  • 1918 : Became the first Indian to be elected a Fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1920 : Died at the age of 32.
